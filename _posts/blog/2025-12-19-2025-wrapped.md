Let's go over my year:

I can divide it chronologically into five parts:

1. University (Jan-Apr)

2. Figuring it out (Apr-May)

3. FR8 Summer (Jun-Sep)

4. Fun September (Sep)

5. At Home (Sep-Dec)

And in order of how satisfied I am with each part:

1. FR8 Summer

2. Fun September

3. University

4. At Home

5. Figuring it out

Let's explain each part succinctly. The first stage of the year was finishing my bachelor of computer science at the University of Alberta and my classes ended at the beginning of April, but I had exams later in the month. In fact I only had one exam which was just a history written exam. The other two courses that I took in computer science were research courses, so there was no exam. And in my English course we just had a final project which we submitted. So studies actually ended start of April. I also didn't mention this, but for the first week of January, I was in Portugal with my family on a trip, and that was such an amazing trip.

Then in stage two, which is to say April to May, I was figuring it out, so to speak, which is to say not doing much and kind of feeling like shit because university was my entire life for the past four years at that point and then it was all gone, even though I had the option to do a masters full ride at that point, I ended up not accepting it that summer. But yeah, at that time I was just really unsure of what I wanted and pretty depressed. You can see in the appendix that from March 29th to May 25th, I did not track any habits. It's also worth mentioning that I injured myself in the knee in February and was not running until May, which affected me negatively. The godforsaken trip to vancouver also happened in this time.

Of course, FR8 was the highlight of the year because I was in an environment where everywhere you looked it was insanely talented, people with high ambition, which is also what I consider myself. However, I need to be in an environment where I can socialize and be happy and interact with others, which is exactly what FR8 allowed me to do. I did a junior reflection on FR8 [here](./2025-10-18-experience.md), but I hope to reflect on it further in the future.

Then we had fun September, which started off in the beginning of September with a trip to Estonia with my sister where we met Jan and that was amazing, even though I was actually sick at that time and drinking water like a maniac. Then I was in Ottawa for 10 days for my cousin's wedding and reconnected with some friends there as well as biked a lot like I did the summer prior and then from the 15th to the 24th about I was back in Edmonton where I hanged out with some friends, did a nice hundred fifty kilometer bike ride with my friend Dev, and prepared for my bike trip through the Rockies, which was from the 24th to the 29th. And that was the highlight of the year because it was insanely difficult. And brutal, but I survived. I also went to another wedding, which this time was in Edmonton with one of our family friends, and that was amazing. It was actually the day after the 150-kilometer bike ride, so I ate the entire place

Then for the remainder of the year, I was at home working remotely for a startup and that was a big mistake. I literally knew that working remotely would not be ideal, but I still accepted the job even though I had other options in front of me. The good news is that come January, I'll be leaving. During this period of time though, I spent time with family and we went to many different gatherings for Thanksgiving, Christmas, etcetera. So that was really nice. And I also got to spend a lot of time with my uncle since we went on daily walks for the better part of September to November.

Here are my raw and somewhat naive goals for 2025 that I wrote in apple notes. For goals that are habits, I track them in a Google sheet. So if that goal has a corresponding entry in my habit tracker, then I won't comment on it. But you can check the appendix to see it.

- Meditation
- Move your neck and head while seated and stretch your lower back  
  > didn't ever really do this consciously. Sometimes when it came to my mind that I was sitting at my desk for too long, I started stretching my neck, but otherwise it wasn't really consistent. And the most intentional way that I could do this is by planning my day a priori so that I have to take a break between deep work sessions anyways
- Morning and evening schedules
  > this was a fail: I did it only when I was motivated to, but not with discipline
- Deep work
  > ditto schedules
- Express gratitude
- Maintain good posture with a straight gaze
  > ditto move neck
- Stop drinking water before sleep
  > The intent was this was to prevent myself from waking up to go pee in the middle of the night. And I did this with varying like levels of success. Sometimes in the middle of the night be f sometimes in the evening I'd be thirsty and I'd say, well, screw it, I'm gonna go drink water. water because I'm thirsty, but on the whole having this in mind definitely prevented me from interrupting sleep more than not having it in my mind.
- Sleep from 9:00 p.m. to 5:00 a.m.
  > My sleep was overall great 2025. It wasn't perfect, but it was good. And the one thing is when I was in Finland near the summer solstice, there's no chance you could fall asleep at 9 p.m. because of the sun. So it's more of like a seasonal variation where in the winter I can easily sleep at 8 p. but in the summer I can't. So I don't think there should be a specific time frame to sleep. It should just be sleep early and sleep for eight hours. And for most of the year I got that done.
- Keep your smartphone always stored far from the desk
  > Sucess: while at FR8 I rarely brought my phone to my desk and at home I leave it in the mudroom
- Enter monk mode every time you put your phone away
  > yeah, this was unrealistic
- No YouTube (only for educational purposes while taking notes like Hugo) or social media
  > this never happened
- Be very selfish with your time
  > hard to quantify
- Bet on yourself
  > ditto selfish
- Connect with deep learning researchers and engineers via X
  > yes at the beginning of the year, then I lost interest in X since it's bad
- Run marathons, even ultra-marathons
  > I did both. I ran the Helsinki Marathon officially and also did a 50km ultra marathon.
- Integrate GPT into language maintenance and flashcards
  > yes to GPT, flashcards no
- Record your learnings every day on RemNote
  > I succeeded in saving them daily but not in practicing them
- Be more honest with myself and with others
  > hard to quantify, need to re-name these goals for next year to be quantifiable
